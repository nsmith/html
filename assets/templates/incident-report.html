INC-2025-0731

Elevated checkout errors during payment processing

SEV-2 Resolved

Detected 09:14 UTC on 31 Jul 2025 · Incident lead: A. Rivera · Last updated 11:02 UTC

At a glance

Severity
SEV-2
Impact
~6.8% of checkouts
Duration
38 min

Summary

A routine deploy of the payments service shipped a misconfigured retry policy that exhausted the connection pool to the payment gateway. From 09:14 to 09:52 UTC, roughly 6.8% of checkout attempts returned 503 errors. Service recovered after the change was rolled back and the pool was reset. No payments were double-charged and no customer data was affected.

Timeline (all times UTC)

  • 09:08
    Payments service v2.41.0 begins rolling out to production.
  • 09:14
    Error-rate alert fires; checkout 503 rate crosses 5%. On-call paged.
  • 09:21
    Investigation begins. Initial suspicion falls on the gateway provider (later ruled out).
  • 09:33
    Connection-pool saturation identified in dashboards; correlated with the 09:08 deploy.
  • 09:41
    Rollback of v2.41.0 initiated; pool drained and restarted.
  • 09:52
    Error rate returns to baseline. Incident declared resolved after 15 min of stability.

Root cause

The new release set the gateway client's max_retries to 10 with no backoff, intended only as a local test value. Under normal traffic this multiplied in-flight requests roughly tenfold during transient errors, exhausting the fixed-size connection pool (capacity 64).

Contributing factors: the test value was not caught in review, and load tests run in staging used a smaller traffic profile that never saturated the pool.
